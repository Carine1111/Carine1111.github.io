Favorite Places in Europe

Amsterdam

Italy

I'll admit that I'm slightly biased when it comes to liking Amsterdam since I'm 1/4 Dutch. However, even if you aren't Dutch, you are sure to enjoy Amsterdam between their tourist attractions and delicious food. One of my absolute favorite places there is Keukenhof, which is basically a giant tulip garden (first picture in the column). The grounds are vast and filled with flowers of all colors, perfect for taking a peaceful walk or taking photos in. You can also rent bikes to ride around the fields as a fun way to add in a little exercise. On another note, their stroopwafels and pannenkoeken are phenomenal. I mean, who wouldn't love two wafels stuck together with caramel-like syrup (stroop) or the Dutch take on a pancake?

Just like in Amsterdam, the food in Italy is to die for. There may be plenty of Italian restaurants in your home town, but nothing can beat eating fresh pasta while admiring the beautiful landscapes of Sardinia, Piedmont, or wherever you choose to visit. There are also tons of fun activites you can do while you're there, like hiking, biking, and skiing in the wintertime. When we visited the Dolomites, we also took a tour of some World War I tunnel and trench networks, whcih were very cool.
